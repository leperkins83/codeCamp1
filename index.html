Timeline

  • 3 January 1892- born in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State, Africa.
  • 1895- Tolkien family moved to England; his father, Arthur Ruel Tolkien, died of rheumatic fever back in Africa.
  • 1896- family moved to Sarehole, Worcestershire (later annexed into Birmingham), England; exploring the countryside provided many inspiration for the Shire, as well as his Aunt Jane's farm, Bag End.
  • 1904- mother Mabel Tolkien dies of acute diabetes.
  • 1909- fell in love with Edith Mary Bratt.
  • 1911-1914- formed the T.C.B.S. club with several classmates; reinforced Tolkien's dedication to poetry.
  • 8 January 1913- Tolkien and Edith become engaged.
  • 1915- graduated from Exeter College, Oxford in Literature.
  • 15 July 1915- commissioned into the British Army.
  • 22 March 1916- Tolkien and Edith married.
  • 2 June 1916- sent to France, World War I.
  • July 1916-October 1916- fought in the trenches of Somme, France.
  • November 1916- sent back to England due to trench fever.
  • 1917-1918- Began writing The Book of Lost Tales, beginning with The Fall of Gondolin.
  • 1920- demobilized and left the Army.
  • 1925- worked at Oxford as the Rawlinson and Bosworth Professor of Anglo-Saxon.
  • 1926- translated Beowulf.
  • 1938- published The Hobbit.
  • 1954-1955- The Lord of the Rings published.
  • 29 November 1971- Edith Tolkien passed away.
  • 2 September 1973- J.R.R. Tolkien passed away from pneumonia.
